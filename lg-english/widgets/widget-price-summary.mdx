The `price summary` widget shows the most important prices for the set or minifig according to its `new` or `used` status. 

If you activate the `shops fees` parameter in the [settings](/lg-english/settings), the prices shown take into account an **estimate** of the `fees` payable by buyers of your products on sales platforms that charge fees to buyers, such as Vinted or Leboncoin.

## Exemples

With the `shops fees` parameter deactivated, the `discount` parameter = 0.9 and the `margin new` parameter = 0.3:

Let's take a `new` item with `market price` = 100.00<br />.
- To be competitive, you can sell it at `market price` * `discount`. So `selling price` = 100.00 * 0.90 = **90.00**.
- To obtain a `margin new` of 30%, you must buy it at `selling price` / (1+`margin new`). So `purchase price` = 90.00 / (1+0.3) = **69.23**
- You'll make a `profit` of `selling price` - `purchase price`. So `profit` = 90.00 - 69.23 = **20.77**
- Or a `profit` / `purchase price` margin. So `margin new` = 20.77 / 69.23 = **0.3**

***
With the `shops fees` parameter activated, the `discount` parameter = 0.9 and the `margin new` parameter = 0.3:

Let's take a `new` item with `market price` = 100.00<br />
- To be competitive, you can sell it at `market price` * `discount` - `fees`. So `selling price` = 100 * 0.9 - 8.58 = **81.42**.
- To obtain a `margin new` of 30%, you need to buy it at `selling price` / (1+`margin new`). So `purchase price` = 81.42 / (1+0.3) = **62.63**
- It must therefore be displayed in the store where you are going to buy it at `purchase price` - `fees`. So `listed price` = 62.63 - 6.71 = **55.92**
- You will make a `profit` of `selling price` - `purchase price`. So `profit` = 81.42 - 63.63 = **18.79**
- Or a `profit` / `purchase price` margin. So `margin new` = 18.79 / 62.63 = **0.3**
